White Tank Mountain

Welcome to my mini tour of White Tank Mountain. I'm your host, Alejandra. My tour is an example from my my portfolio of instructional design and graphic design projects. Now, I will take you on a tour of the White Tank Mountains. Get your hiking shoes and come with me!

All of the images for this webpage were taken by me, Alejandra Dashe, at White Tank Mountain Regional Park. You can visit White Tank Mountain by following the map linked below.


Things to Do: Hiking


You can take a hike in the fresh air using the map depicted on the right side of this text. It is important that you get your bearings before you start the hike. Using the map will help you find your way to the waterfall at the end of the trail.

Things to See: Petroglyphs


The White Tank Mountain Regional Park was once occupied by Hohokam Indians. While walking along the trail, you can see petroglyphs in the picture of the right. Petroglyphs are writings on stones. More information: Maricopa County Parks.

Things to Appreciate: Plants


As you hike, you will find yourself appreciating the beauty of the different colors of the green desert. On the right is a picture of a flowering cactus. It's always a surprise to see the plants growing from rocks.

Things to Notice: The Waterfall


And now, the best part. The waterfall! Up until this point you have seen beautiful flowers and petroglyphs. The waterfall is at the end of the hike. You can see the water at the bottom of the waterfall, photo on the left.
